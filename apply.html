Join the Fellowship

Applications Open Fall 2026

The Opus Society admits 25 fellows each fall through a competitive application process. We select on merit and potential — not prior connections or existing privilege. If public service is where you're headed, this is where you start.

Who Can Apply

Eligibility Requirements

The Opus Society is designed for first- and second-year students who are early in their Clemson career and have the most to gain from two years of structured professional development. Applications from juniors and seniors will not be considered — the two-year fellowship model requires the full commitment.

Year

First or Second Year

Must be a first-year or sophomore at the time of application. The fellowship is designed around a two-year commitment beginning in Year One.

GPA

3.0 or Higher

A cumulative GPA of 3.0 or higher is required at the time of application. GPA is a floor, not the primary criterion — strong applicants have more going for them than grades alone.

Interest

Public Service Focus

Demonstrated interest in public service, policy, law, or government careers. This doesn't require certainty — it requires genuine curiosity and clear direction.

Commitment

Two Full Years

Applicants must be able to fulfill the two-year fellowship commitment, including meetings, deliverables, and mentorship through the end of their sophomore or junior year.

What to Submit

The Application

The Opus Society application is intentionally substantive but not burdensome. We want to see how you think and where you're headed — not how many hours you spent formatting a document. Give us real answers.

Required

Resume

A current resume reflecting your academic background, activities, work experience, and any relevant interests. Don't worry if it's sparse — you're a first or second year. We're looking at trajectory, not track record.

Required

Personal Statement

A written response addressing your interest in public service and your career goals. We want to understand why you're drawn to this work, what you're curious about, and what you hope to do with it. There's no right answer — but there is a difference between a thoughtful answer and a generic one.

Required

Short Answer Questions

One or more short-answer questions designed to assess your analytical aptitude, professional self-awareness, and fit with the fellowship's values. These are not trick questions. Answer honestly and specifically.

Optional

Letter of Recommendation

An optional letter from a faculty member, employer, supervisor, or mentor who can speak to your professional potential, work ethic, or intellectual character. Not required — but a strong letter from the right person can meaningfully support your application.

Recruitment Calendar

Application Timeline

1

Late August

Applications Open

The application goes live and tabling at Clemson's involvement fair begins. This is also when Opus Society information sessions are held for interested students. Attendance at an info session is not required but is strongly encouraged.

2

Mid-September

Application Deadline

All materials must be submitted by the stated deadline. Late applications will not be considered. The deadline is firm — plan accordingly and don't wait until the last day.

3

Mid to Late September

Review & Interviews

The Director of Recruitment and Executive Director review all applications. Finalists are invited to a brief interview with members of the Executive Board. The interview is conversational — we want to get to know you, not interrogate you.

4

Late September

Decisions Communicated

All applicants — accepted, waitlisted, and not accepted — are notified by email. Accepted fellows receive onboarding information and next steps. We aim to give everyone a decision, not just those who were accepted.

5

October

Fellowship Orientation Begins

The new cohort meets as a group for the first time, mentor matching begins, and Year One programming kicks off. Welcome to Opus.

How We Decide

Selection Criteria

Selection is holistic. We are building a cohort of 25 people who will work closely together for two years — we're looking for the right mix of potential, ambition, diversity of background, and genuine interest in public service. GPA matters, but it is not the primary criterion.

Written Communication

Your personal statement and short answers are the clearest window we have into how you think. Strong applicants write clearly, specifically, and honestly. Generic answers about "wanting to make a difference" will not stand out.

Interest in Public Service

We are not looking for fully formed career plans. We are looking for genuine curiosity about public service careers and evidence — however early — that this is a real direction, not a box you're checking.

Leadership Potential

Evidence of initiative, responsibility, and leadership — in school, work, community, or elsewhere. We are building future public servants. Leadership is part of that profile.

Cohort Diversity

We actively seek diversity of background, career interest, and life experience across the cohort. A cohort of 25 people who are all identical in their goals and backgrounds is a less valuable cohort for everyone.

Academic Standing

The 3.0 GPA requirement is a floor. Strong applicants often exceed it, but a high GPA alone does not make a compelling application. We weigh academic standing alongside everything else.

Interview Performance

For finalists, the interview is an opportunity to bring your application to life. We're assessing professionalism, self-awareness, and genuine enthusiasm — not whether you have the right answers.
