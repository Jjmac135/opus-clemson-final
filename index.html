Our Purpose

Why Opus Exists

No student organization at Clemson currently prepares underclassmen for careers in public service and government. Existing programs serve different audiences. The Opus Society fills that gap — catching students early, before they've missed application windows or assumed those careers weren't available to them.

"The Opus Society prepares high-potential underclassmen for careers in public service, policy, law, and government — building the pipeline that Clemson does not yet have."
  • Excellence We hold our members and ourselves to a high standard of professional and intellectual rigor.
  • Service We are oriented toward public good, not private gain.
  • Access We exist specifically to open doors for students who don't yet know those doors exist.
  • Selectivity Our value to members depends on our standards. We are a fellowship, not a club.
  • Continuity We build something that outlasts any individual cohort or executive board.

Fellowship Structure

A Two-Year Journey

The Opus Society operates on a structured two-year model. Year One builds the professional foundation; Year Two translates preparation into real outcomes.

Year One

Foundation Track

Building the professional floor — the skills, vocabulary, and awareness to compete for public service opportunities.

  • Public service career landscape overview
  • Resume & LinkedIn tailored for government and policy roles
  • Federal internship and fellowship pipeline introduction
  • Networking etiquette and professional communication
  • 1-on-1 mentorship with an Executive Board member
  • One substantive deliverable per semester

Year Two

Accelerator Track

Translating preparation into outcomes — developing a career niche and building a real professional network.

  • Career track specialization with tailored programming
  • Competitive fellowship application support (Truman, Rangel, Pickering)
  • Policy memo and briefing document writing workshops
  • Alumni and professional speaker series
  • Washington, D.C. or state capital exposure trip (funding permitting)
  • Capstone deliverable: policy brief or professional portfolio

Specialization

Career Tracks

Beginning in Year Two, fellows select a career track that shapes their mentorship, deliverables, and professional connections.

Law & Justice

Preparation for public interest law, civil rights, federal prosecution, and policy litigation. Emphasis on legal writing and advocacy.

Public defender · Civil rights attorney · Federal prosecutor · ACLU · DOJ

Government & Policy

Preparation for federal agency work, congressional staffing, think tanks, and nonpartisan accountability roles. Emphasis on policy analysis and memo writing.

Congressional staffer · GAO analyst · Federal agency · Think tank researcher

International Affairs

Preparation for diplomacy, national security, and international development. Emphasis on foreign policy analysis and area knowledge.

Foreign Service · State Department · International NGO · National security

Leadership

Executive Board

The Opus Society is student-led and student-run, governed by an Executive Board responsible for organizational strategy, program delivery, member selection, and institutional continuity.

President

Overall organizational leadership, external representation, faculty advisor liaison, and final authority on strategic decisions.

Vice President

Supports the President in day-to-day operations, steps in during absences, and helps coordinate across the executive board.

Director of Recruitment & Mentorship

Manages the application process, campus outreach, and cohort selection; coordinates mentor–fellow matching and alumni relations.

Director of Programming & Logistics

Designs and executes all fellowship programming; coordinates Year One and Year Two tracks; manages the deliverable and event calendar.

Director of Communication

Manages the fellowship's brand, social media presence, internal communications, and outreach to campus partners and prospective fellows.

Faculty Advisor

Serves as the organization's institutional liaison with Clemson, providing continuity across annual leadership transitions and guidance on program design. Attends one meeting per semester and is available for occasional consultation. Time commitment is intentionally light.
